\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Reasoning models such as DeepSeek-R1~\cite{deepseekr1} and QwQ~\cite{qwq} can ``self-jailbreak'': their chain of thought transitions from safety-aware deliberation to committed harmful generation without adversarial input~\cite{harmthoughts,selfjailbreak}. We define the first reasoning step after which safety deliberation permanently ceases as the \textbf{Safety Commitment Point} (CP). Existing safety detectors operate at the token or input level~\cite{trajguard,safeswitch,saferemind}, missing the gradual, multi-step commitment process within reasoning traces. Hidden-state (HS) probing offers a promising alternative for step-level monitoring, but requires choosing which transformer layer to read, a researcher degree of freedom typically treated as an implementation detail. How sensitive are safety probe conclusions to this methodological choice?\looseness=-1

We show this choice alone can reverse a study's central conclusion (\cref{fig:concept_overview}). A crossing-rate composite metric systematically selects shallow layers (L0--L10), while threshold-based metrics select deep layers (L14--L63), producing a 14-to-61-layer divergence across all four models. At deep layers, HS probes show cross-validated precision advantages of +20--22pp at 7--8B; at 32B, results are suggestive but limited by small test sets ($n \leq 20$). The mechanism is cosine compression: shallow-layer representations compress directional information, inflating crossing-rate metrics near the noise floor (\cref{fig:cosine_compression}). We study four models across two architectures and two scales (\cref{tab:models}).\looseness=-1

Our contributions are threefold. \textbf{(1)~Metric-induced layer selection bias}: crossing-rate composite metrics systematically select shallow layers, producing a qualitative reversal of the hidden-state advantage; this bias generalizes across models, datasets (AdvBench), and a fifth architecture (Gemma-2). \textbf{(2)~Boundary conditions on the hidden-state advantage}: cross-validated at 7--8B, conditional on encoder quality, and suggestive at 32B. \textbf{(3)~Negative results}: seven alternative methods all fail, while control tasks confirm genuine safety structure.\looseness=-1
